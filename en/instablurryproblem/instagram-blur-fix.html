Why Are My Instagram Stories Blurry? (2025 Fix)

Feb 07, 2025 ‚Ä¢ Tech ‚Ä¢ 4 min read

You take a crystal clear photo, but as soon as you upload it to your Story, it looks like it was taken with a toaster. This issue predominantly affects Android users. Why does Instagram seemingly favor iPhone?

The "Screen Record" Problem

It's not your camera, it's the compression. Because there are thousands of Android devices, Instagram can't optimize for every single camera lens. Instead, the app essentially takes a "screenshot" of your viewfinder, which drastically reduces quality.

‚ö° Solution 1: The Hidden "High Quality" Toggle

Instagram disables high-quality uploads by default to save data. Most users don't even know this setting exists. To fix this:

  • Go to your Profile and tap the 3 Lines menu.
  • Select Settings and privacy > Data usage and media quality.
  • Toggle "Upload at highest quality" to ON.

Solution 2: The Aspect Ratio Mistake

Most phone cameras shoot in a 4:3 ratio by default. However, Instagram Stories are 9:16 (Full Screen).

If you upload a 4:3 photo, Instagram has to digitally zoom in to fill the screen. Digital zoom = Pixelation.

The Fix: Open your native camera app settings and change the photo aspect ratio to 9:16 (Full) before shooting for Stories.

Solution 3: Don't Use the Instagram Camera

As mentioned, the in-app camera is poorly optimized on Android. For the crispest results:

  • Take the photo/video with your phone's native Camera app (Samsung/Google/Xiaomi app).
  • Edit it there if needed.
  • Open Instagram and upload it from your gallery.

Conclusion

By enabling high-quality uploads and shooting in the correct aspect ratio, you can achieve 95% of the quality of an iPhone upload. It just takes a few extra seconds of preparation.
