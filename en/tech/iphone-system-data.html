How to Fix iPhone "System Data" Storage Filling Up (2026)

Feb 10, 2026 â€¢ Tech â€¢ 4 min read

You deleted thousands of photos and uninstalled apps, but your iPhone storage is still full? When you check settings, you see the grey "System Data" (formerly "Other") taking up 20-30 GB of space.

This data consists of cache files, Siri voices, and failed updates. Apple doesn't provide a delete button, but we know how to clear it.

ðŸ§¹ Solution 1: Clear Safari Cache (Main Culprit)

Cookies and video caches from browsing pile up here.

  1. Go to Settings > Safari.
  2. Scroll down and tap "Clear History and Website Data".
  3. This usually frees up 3-5 GB instantly. Note: It closes your open tabs but keeps your passwords.

Solution 2: The Date Trick (Secret Method)

This trick forces iOS to delete "expired" log files.

  • Go to Settings > General > Date & Time.
  • Turn off "Set Automatically".
  • Change the date to 1 year in the future.
  • Go back to home screen and wait for 1 minute. Then restart your phone.
  • After restart, set the date back to "Automatic". The system will have cleared the "old" data.

Solution 3: Review Large Attachments

GIFs and videos from years ago might still be on your phone.

  • Go to Settings > General > iPhone Storage.
  • Find and tap on Messages.
  • Tap "Review Large Attachments" and delete old videos.
